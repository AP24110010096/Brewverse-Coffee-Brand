Editorial • Issue 012

7 Reasons Your Morning
Cup Is Lying to You.

Everything India's instant coffee industry doesn't want you to figure out — and what to do about it.

expand_more
01
The Preservation Lie

Instant Isn't "Fresh" – It's Processed Debris

Most commercial instant coffee brands rely on freeze-drying technology that strips the bean of its natural essential oils. What you're drinking isn't coffee; it's the ghost of a bean, resurrected with artificial acidity.

02
Origin Obscurity

The "Single Origin" Shell Game

Mass producers often blend inferior Robusta beans with high-grade Arabica to cut costs, masking the bitter finish with heavy roasting profiles. True transparency means knowing the specific farm, not just the country.

03
Roasting Ethics

The Charred Cover-Up

A dark roast shouldn't taste like ash. Large-scale roasters push temperatures too high to mask defects in low-quality beans, creating a uniform, burnt flavor profile that obscures the unique terroir of the coffee.

04
Bloom Secrets

If it doesn't bloom, it's dead.

The "bloom" is the release of CO2 when hot water hits fresh grounds. If your morning coffee remains flat and static, it's a sign the beans were roasted months or even years ago.

05
Shelf Life Deception

Oxygen: The Silent Thief

Pre-ground coffee loses up to 60% of its aroma within 15 minutes of being exposed to air. Commercial bags on supermarket shelves have often been oxidized for weeks before they reach your kitchen.

06
The Water Myth

Boiling Water is Killing the Soul

Pouring 100°C water directly onto coffee grounds scours the delicate flavor notes, leaving only the bitter, harsh chemical compounds. The sommelier approach requires a precise 92-96°C range.

07
The Grinder Gap

Blade Grinders are Sensory Sabotage

Blade grinders smash beans into uneven chunks, causing uneven extraction. A burr grinder—the tool of the sommelier—crushes beans with surgical precision to unlock the hidden sugar browning notes.
