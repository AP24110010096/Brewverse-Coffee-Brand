Common Enquiries

What makes Brewverse different from regular instant coffee? +

Most instant coffee is made from low-grade Robusta beans, spray-dried to strip out anything interesting. Brewverse uses origin-specific beans with flavor profiles you can actually name — Madagascar vanilla, wild hazelnut — processed to preserve the taste, not just the caffeine. It's instant in convenience, not in compromise.

Is Brewverse coffee healthy? What's in it? +

No artificial flavors. No added sugar. No preservatives. The flavor you taste — vanilla, hazelnut — comes from real ingredient sourcing, not a lab. Clean inputs, clean energy. If you're currently drinking sugar-loaded café drinks for the "coffee experience," Brewverse is a direct upgrade.

How do I brew the perfect cup at home? +

1–1.5 teaspoons in 150ml of hot water at 85–90°C. Stir for 20 seconds. Don't use boiling water — it kills the nuance. Want it creamier? Swap water for warm milk. That's all there is to it.

How is Brewverse priced compared to café coffee? +

A single café cup in a Tier 1 city runs ₹150–₹300. A Brewverse jar gives you 15–20 cups. Do that math and you'll understand why "premium taste without premium pricing" isn't just a tagline — it's the whole point. Exact pricing is listed on the product pages.

Where are the beans sourced from? +

We source from specific origins chosen for their distinct flavor profiles — Madagascar for the vanilla character in Vanilla Velvet, for example. Every product has a traceable origin story. You'll find the sourcing detail on each product's page.

How long does one jar last? +

Roughly 15–20 cups per jar depending on how strong you brew. For a once-a-day drinker, that's two to three weeks of mornings — at a fraction of what the same habit costs at a café.
