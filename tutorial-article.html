Step by Step

The Process

01
Phase One

The Grind Foundation

A medium-coarse texture resembling sea salt is paramount. Too fine, and the water stalls; too coarse, and the extraction lacks the soul of the bean. Grind moments before the first drop hits.

☕ Target: 30g Beans
02
Phase Two

The Sacred Bloom

Introduce 60g of water at 94°C. Watch as the gases escape, creating a bubbling landscape. This 30-second pause awakens the aromatics and prepares the bed for a uniform extraction.

⏳ Duration: 45 Seconds
03
Phase Three

Concentric Flow

Maintain a steady, pencil-thin stream. Move in concentric circles from the center outwards, never touching the paper walls. The goal is a flat coffee bed once the water has passed.

🌍 Yield: 500ml Liquid Gold
☕
Sommelier Insight

The Temperature Trap

"Boiling water is the enemy of nuance. If you don't have a temperature-controlled kettle, wait exactly 90 seconds after boiling before you pour. Those few degrees are the difference between a smoky bitterness and a bright, berry acidity."
